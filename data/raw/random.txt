[EXECUTIVE SUMMARY]
As of March 31, 2026, the conflict between the United States/Israel coalition and 
the Islamic Republic of Iran has entered a phase of "Infrastructure Attrition." 
Following the assassination of Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei on Day 1, the Iranian 
regime has reorganized under Mojtaba Khamenei. Despite losing significant 
integrated air defense systems (IADS) and command-and-control nodes, Iran 
continues to utilize asymmetric naval tactics in the Strait of Hormuz and 
ballistic missile strikes against regional US partners. 

Global oil prices have stabilized at $145/barrel following the partial closure 
of the Strait, though rationing is now in effect across several East Asian 
nations. Diplomacy via Pakistani intermediaries remains stalled.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[DAY-BY-DAY CHRONOLOGY: MARCH 2026]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

MARCH 01: THE VACUUM
Following the massive initial strikes of "Operation Epic Fury," Tehran is in 
chaos. US Central Command (CENTCOM) confirms the death of Supreme Leader 
Ali Khamenei. President Trump urges the Iranian public to "reclaim their 
country." Iran’s Assembly of Experts goes underground.

MARCH 02: PROXY ACTIVATION
Hezbollah launches a saturation attack of 2,000+ rockets into Northern Israel 
to relieve pressure on Tehran. The US carrier strike group USS Gerald R. Ford 
begins intercepting drones in the Eastern Mediterranean.

MARCH 03: THE SMOKE OVER TEHRAN
A second wave of joint US-Israeli strikes targets the "Grey Zone"—IRGC 
leadership bunkers in northern Tehran. Large plumes of smoke are visible 
via satellite over the capital. Global markets panic; Brent Crude hits $120.

MARCH 04: THE STRAIT TIGHTENS
Iran’s Navy (IRIN) begins mining the narrowest points of the Strait of Hormuz. 
Two commercial tankers (Liberian and Marshall Islands flagged) are damaged by 
limpet mines. Insurance rates for Gulf shipping soar by 400%.

MARCH 05: SUCCESSION
The Iranian state media announces Mojtaba Khamenei as the new Supreme Leader. 
In his first address, he vows "eternal resistance" and rejects any 
negotiation with the "Great Satan."

MARCH 06: INFRASTRUCTURE HITS
US B-2 bombers strike Iranian drone manufacturing plants in Isfahan and 
Kashan. Reports emerge of significant civilian displacement from Tehran 
toward the Caspian Sea coast.

MARCH 07: CYBER BLACKOUT
"Operation Ledger" is launched by US Cyber Command. Iran’s domestic 
banking system and electrical grid in the southern provinces go offline 
for 48 hours. Iran retaliates with a cyberattack on a municipal water 
facility in Florida.

MARCH 08: REGIONAL SPILLOVER
Houthi rebels in Yemen launch ballistic missiles at desalination plants 
in Saudi Arabia. US Patriot batteries intercept 4 out of 5 missiles. One 
hits a storage tank, causing localized water shortages.

MARCH 09: THE "SQUEEZE" POLICY
The US Treasury Department announces a total secondary sanctions regime, 
effectively threatening to cut off any nation (including China) from the 
US dollar if they continue purchasing Iranian "ghost" oil.

MARCH 10: MARITIME SKIRMISH
A US Arleigh Burke-class destroyer engages four IRGC fast-attack craft 
attempting to harass a convoy. All four Iranian vessels are sunk. 
No US casualties reported.

MARCH 11: ISRAELI GROUND BUFFER
The IDF enters Southern Lebanon to establish a 10km "security zone" to 
stop Hezbollah short-range rocket fire. Heavy urban fighting in Tyre.

MARCH 12: TURKISH NEUTRALITY TESTED
NATO forces at Incirlik Air Base intercept Iranian drones targeting US 
assets. Turkey’s President calls for an immediate ceasefire but refuses 
to close airspace to NATO jets.

MARCH 13: THE "GHOST REVOLUTION"
Small-scale protests break out in Mashhad and Tabriz. Protesters 
chant against both the regime and the US strikes. The IRGC responds 
with lethal force; 40+ reported dead.

MARCH 14: THE LONG RANGE THREAT
Iran launches a Fateh-110 missile at a US logistics hub in Qatar. 
The missile is intercepted, but debris causes damage to an 
auxiliary hangar.

MARCH 15: CASUALTY MILESTONE
The UN estimates that the conflict has resulted in over 12,000 
Iranian military casualties and 2,500 civilian deaths due to 
"collateral proximity" to IRGC sites.

MARCH 16: OIL RATIONING BEGINS
Japan and South Korea announce "Emergency Energy Conservation" 
measures. Non-essential driving is restricted.

MARCH 17: DIPLOMATIC LOSS
Ali Larijani, a key pragmatist and former negotiator, is killed 
in an airstrike on a safehouse. This effectively kills the "moderate" 
wing of the Iranian government.

MARCH 18: CHINESE MEDIATION ATTEMPT
Beijing offers to host "Peace Talks for the Gulf." The US State 
Department declines, citing Iran's refusal to stop enrichment 
activities during the conflict.

MARCH 19: THE "SWARM" ATTACK
Iran utilizes hundreds of "suicide drones" in a coordinated 
attack on the Al-Dhafra Air Base (UAE). US C-RAM systems are 
overwhelmed; two F-35s are damaged on the tarmac.

MARCH 20: RED SEA ESCALATION
The Houthis successfully hit a commercial cargo ship in the Bab 
el-Mandeb. The vessel sinks. The Red Sea is now considered 
a "High Risk" zone for all commercial traffic.

MARCH 21: NOWRUZ (PERSIAN NEW YEAR)
The usual celebrations are replaced by state-mandated mourning. 
Mojtaba Khamenei appears in a video from an undisclosed location, 
claiming the "Final War" has begun.

MARCH 22: THE HORMUZ DECREE
Iran officially declares the Strait of Hormuz closed to all 
"hostile" vessels. Non-hostile ships must pay a "transit tax" 
to the IRGC to pass. The US Navy calls this "illegal piracy."

MARCH 23: RUSSIAN INVOLVEMENT
Reports indicate Russia is providing "Intelligence and 
Electronic Warfare" support to Tehran via their bases in Syria. 
The US issues a formal warning to Moscow.

MARCH 24: THE 15-POINT PROPOSAL
Via Pakistan, the US sends a final ultimatum: Total 
denuclearization, cessation of proxy support, and recognition 
of the new maritime borders in exchange for a ceasefire.

MARCH 25: THE REJECTION
Tehran rejects the proposal within 4 hours. Israel's Netanyahu 
orders the IDF to "decapitate" the remainder of Iran’s arms industry. 
Strikes hit Bandar Abbas port facilities.

MARCH 26: JORDAN TARGETED
Iran-backed militias in Iraq fire drones at a US transit hub in 
Jordan. This is a significant escalation as Jordan had been 
the primary corridor for civilian evacuations.

MARCH 27: THE REFUGEE CRISIS
The UNHCR reports 200,000+ people have crossed from Lebanon 
into Syria to escape the Israeli-Hezbollah front.

MARCH 28: KHARG ISLAND THREAT
The White House warns that if one more US base is hit, 
Iran’s Kharg Island oil terminal—their last economic lifeline—
will be "erased from the map."

MARCH 29: KUWAIT STRIKE
An Iranian missile hits a desalination and power plant in 
Kuwait. An Indian worker is killed. This brings international 
condemnation from New Delhi.

MARCH 30: THE TRUMP ULTIMATUM
President Trump issues a Sunday deadline: "Open the Strait or 
we take Kharg Island." Israel reports a missile hit on a 
Haifa refinery; Iran claims responsibility.

MARCH 31: CURRENT STATUS (TODAY)
Centcom reports "Operation Epic Fury" is transitioning to 
Special Operations. 50,000+ US troops are now in the theater. 
The White House is touting a "limited" Hormuz passage for 
allies, but the risk of total regional war is at its highest 
level since 1945.
